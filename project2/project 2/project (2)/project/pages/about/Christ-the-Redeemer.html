Christ the Redeemer Corcovado mountain, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
Christ the Redeemer is an iconic statue overlooking Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, atop the Corcovado mountain. Standing at 98 feet tall, its outstretched arms span 92 feet, symbolizing peace and protection. Completed in 1931, it has become a global symbol of Christianity and Brazilian culture, attracting millions of visitors annually. The statue's art deco design and breathtaking panoramic views make it a marvel of engineering and artistry. Christ the Redeemer stands as a testament to faith and human achievement, embodying the unity of spirituality and natural beauty in one of the world's most vibrant cities.
