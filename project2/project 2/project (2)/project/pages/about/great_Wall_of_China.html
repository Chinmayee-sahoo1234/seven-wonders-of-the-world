Great Wall of China China,1987
The Great Wall of China, spanning over 13,000 miles, is an ancient marvel of architecture and defense. Built over several centuries by various Chinese dynasties, it served as a formidable barrier against invasions from nomadic tribes. Originally constructed of stone, brick, tamped earth, and other materials, its purpose was to protect Chinese states and empires from raids and incursions. Parts of the wall are remarkably well-preserved, offering visitors a glimpse into China's rich history and engineering prowess. Today, it stands as one of the most iconic symbols of Chinese civilization and is a UNESCO World Heritage site drawing millions of tourists annually.
