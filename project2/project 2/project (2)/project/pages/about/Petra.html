Petra Ma'an Governorate, Jordan
Petra, a UNESCO World Heritage site in Jordan, is an ancient city carved into rose-colored sandstone cliffs. Flourishing as the capital of the Nabataean kingdom from 4th century BC to 2nd century AD, it was a vital hub for trade routes linking Arabia, Egypt, and Syria. The city features tombs, temples, and a Roman-style amphitheater, showcasing a blend of Nabataean, Greco-Roman, and Arabian influences. Rediscovered in the 19th century, Petra continues to captivate visitors with its stunning beauty and historical significance.
