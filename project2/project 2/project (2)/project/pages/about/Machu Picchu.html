Machu Picchu Cusco Region, Peru
Machu Picchu, perched high in the Peruvian Andes, is an ancient Incan citadel renowned for its breathtaking mountain vistas and architectural marvels. Built in the 15th century and abandoned a century later, it remained hidden until its rediscovery in 1911 by Hiram Bingham. This UNESCO World Heritage site is celebrated for its sophisticated stone masonry, terraced fields, and intricate urban planning, reflecting its dual role as a royal retreat and religious sanctuary. The Intihuatana stone, believed to have astronomical significance, and the Temple of the Sun are among its notable structures, making Machu Picchu a pinnacle of Incan civilization and a wonder of the world.
