The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an iconic symbol of love and architectural splendor. Commissioned in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as a masterpiece of Mughal art and design. Crafted from white marble adorned with intricate inlay work of semi-precious stones, the Taj Mahal mesmerizes with its symmetrical beauty and majestic domed mausoleum. Surrounding gardens, reflecting pools, and ornate mosques enhance its grandeur, reflecting an era of artistic achievement and cultural synthesis. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, the Taj Mahal continues to captivate millions with its timeless elegance.
